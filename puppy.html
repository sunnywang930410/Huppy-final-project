Learn the growth process of puppy

Born

During the first stages of growth, puppies spend most of their time eating or sleeping. This is very important for good healthy development

Neonatal

The majority of a puppy's life in this first stage of growth is spent feeding or sleeping. This is vital to healthy development, and care should be taken to avoid disturbing the litter or the mother.

Weaning

First steps are taken and puppies begin to play fight with their siblings, and may even begin to practice growling and tail-wagging as social interaction begins.

Puppyhood

This is the stage where structure, activity and boundary reaffirmation are vital. Puppies have a natural capacity for learning at this age, so it's important that lessons learned during this time need to be adhered to, and training and wider socialization needs to be maintained.

Adult

The growth process for puppies differs depending on their breed size. Extra Small breed dogs can reach full maturity at 8 months, while giant breeds are not considered fully grown until 2 years old.

Adapt to the new home

Keep Your House Calm

Your puppy may be feeling stressed by the new sights, sounds, and smells, and the separation from their mother. So keep your house very calm to avoid adding to this stress.

Take them outside

Take your puppy to outside area so they can start potty training. If they manage to go, reward them with a positive tone in your voice.

Training

Sit Down

Start by holding the dog treats and placing your hand in front of its nose. Raise the treat above the dog's head and guide them to sit. When dogs raise their heads with treats, they usually sit down. Reward the dog for good behavior.

Lie Down

Once the puppy has learned to sit down, you can teach them to lie down by holding the treat down between the dog's paws and moving them away from the dog a little bit. After the puppy is lying down, it is great to be rewarded with treats.
